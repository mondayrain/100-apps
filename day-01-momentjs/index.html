Timestamps

Since we need to pass time between different timezones, time is usually passed around in UTC (Universal Time, Coordinated) time stamps. UTC is sometimes also referred to as Greenwich Mean Time since it's based on the local standard time in Greenwich. Midnight in Greenwich means 00:00UTC, and noon at 12:00UTC. In the computing world, we also tend to pass around what we call Unix Timestamps. A Unix timestamp (also known as POSIX time) describes the # of seconds that have elapsed since 00:00:00 Thursday, 1 Jan 1970 UTC.

If the date is before 1970, then the time stamp will be negative!

Extra Notes

Daylight savings does not affect UTC; it decides the change in offset to UTC.

Leap seconds for UTC is kept within 0.9 seconds of physical reality by introducing a "leap second" in the last minute of the UTC year (or the last minute of June).

Remember that UTC is totally arbitary!
